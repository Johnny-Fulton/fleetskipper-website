# Fleetskipper — Networking & Outreach Plan

_UK focus. Target: workboat operators following Workboat Code 3 (WBC3); next wave: fishing vessels._

**Companion file:** `fleetskipper-outreach-tracker.xlsx` (prospect list, ecosystem contacts, pipeline dashboard).

## 1. Who you're really talking to

Your ICP (ideal customer profile) isn't "maritime." Narrow it down so every message lands:

- **Company:** UK workboat operator, 3–50 vessels, operating under WBC3. Mostly CTV/offshore wind support, survey, harbour services, and marine contracting.
- **Person:** Operations Director, Fleet Manager, Designated Person Ashore (DPA), Safety/Compliance Manager, or owner/MD at smaller outfits.
- **Their pain:** Mountains of WBC3 paperwork; crew certs scattered across spreadsheets; planned maintenance tracked on paper; evidencing compliance during MCA audits; chasing skippers for voyage records. They know it's inefficient; most "solutions" are expensive enterprise FMS platforms built for big shipping, not 24m workboats.

**Your elevator line (first draft — tweak for how you actually position):**

> "Fleetskipper is a WBC3-ready operations app built by a pilot for the workboat guys — one place for your crew certs, maintenance, voyage records, and audit evidence, without the enterprise price tag."

> If I've got your positioning wrong, correct it and I'll revise the templates below.

## 2. The five-phase plan

### Phase 1 — Foundation (this week)

Make sure the shopfront is ready before you send anyone to it.

- Your LinkedIn profile headline: "Founder, Fleetskipper | Maritime pilot building the ops app for WBC3 workboats."
- Your LinkedIn "About" section tells the story in 3 short paragraphs: who you are, why you built it, what it does, who it's for.
- The site has a clear "Book a demo" or "Join the pilot" CTA above the fold and a 60-second explainer.
- You have one-pager PDF (1 page, 3 bullets, 2 screenshots) you can attach in emails.
- You've joined the **National Workboat Association** as a supplier/associate member — the single highest-leverage move you can make.

### Phase 2 — Warm-up (week 2)

Don't cold-pitch first. Get seen in the spaces your prospects live in.

- Follow every company in the Prospects tab on LinkedIn.
- Follow the NWA, MCA, Maritime UK, Seawork, Workboat World, Maritime Journal, 4C Offshore.
- Spend 15 min/day liking and thoughtfully commenting on posts from ops directors and fleet managers. Add value — don't pitch.
- Publish 2 LinkedIn posts: one "why I built this" founder story, one practical post (e.g., "3 things MCA surveyors actually check for under WBC3").

### Phase 3 — Targeted outreach (weeks 3+)

Small volume, high quality. Ten a week, not a hundred.

- Each week: pick 10 companies from the Prospects tab (your A-priorities first).
- For each one, find the right person (see "Finding the right contact" below).
- Send a personalised LinkedIn connection request. After they accept, send the message (templates below).
- Same day, send the email variant as a backup touch.
- Follow up 7–10 days later if no response. Two follow-ups max, then move them to Nurture and revisit in 60 days.

### Phase 4 — Ecosystem presence (weeks 4–12)

Events and media create "inbound" — people contacting you.

- **Seawork International** (Southampton, June) — attend as a visitor this year; plan a booth or sponsored slot next year.
- **CTV Summit** — attend; target CTV ops folks.
- Pitch **Workboat World**, **Maritime Journal**, and **Marine Industry News** a founder story or an opinion piece on what WBC3 really demands on the ground.
- Apply to speak at NWA events once you have 2–3 real customers.

### Phase 5 — Referrals & community (weeks 8+)

Once you have even one paying customer, referrals become your best channel.

- Ask every happy user: "Who else in the sector should I talk to?"
- Offer a 2-month discount for referrals that convert.
- Build a small "advisory" circle of 3–5 friendly operators who will give you product feedback in exchange for a discount or equity sliver.

## 3. Finding the right contact at each company

Work down this list until you have a name + way to reach them:

1. **LinkedIn search** — search the company name, filter people by "current company." Look for: Ops Director, Fleet Manager, DPA, Safety Manager, MD.
2. **Company website "about" or "team" page** — often lists key staff and emails.
3. **Companies House** — lists directors with their names; useful for small operators where the MD runs it.
4. **Hunter.io or Apollo.io** — find email addresses by name + domain (free tier is enough at your volume).
5. **NWA member directory** — if you join, you get names.
6. **Trade press** — "Seacat Services appoints..." articles are gold.

## 4. Message templates

**Rule:** never send the same message twice. Personalise the first line every time.

### LinkedIn connection request (300-char limit)

> Hi {FirstName}, fellow maritime pro here — pilot-turned-founder building a WBC3 ops app for workboat operators. Would love to connect and swap notes on how {CompanyName} handles audits and crew certs. No pitch.

### LinkedIn follow-up message (after they accept)

> Thanks for connecting, {FirstName}.
>
> Quick context: I'm a pilot, and I got tired of seeing workboat operators drowning in paperwork they don't have the time for. So I've been building **Fleetskipper** — a simple app for WBC3 crew certs, maintenance logs, voyage records and audit evidence. Built *for* small/mid workboat fleets, not enterprise shipping.
>
> We're rolling it out with a couple of operators right now. Would you be open to a 15-minute call to show you what it does and hear what's actually painful about WBC3 at {CompanyName}?
>
> Worst case, you get a look at something new. Best case, we save your DPA a few hours a week.
>
> — Johnny

### Cold email (same person, same day)

> **Subject:** WBC3 ops — quick look from a fellow maritime pro?
>
> Hi {FirstName},
>
> I'm Johnny, a UK pilot who's been building **Fleetskipper** — an operations app aimed squarely at WBC3 workboat operators. One place for crew certs, maintenance, voyage records, and audit evidence, without the enterprise price tag.
>
> I've seen {CompanyName} running {something specific about them — fleet size / recent contract / vessel}. If a 15-minute call makes sense to see if Fleetskipper might save your team time on WBC3 admin, I'd welcome it.
>
> If not now, all good — happy to stay in touch.
>
> Best,
> Johnny
> Founder, Fleetskipper
> [website] | [LinkedIn] | [phone]

### Follow-up (7–10 days later)

> Hi {FirstName}, just floating this back up in case it got lost. No pressure — happy to send a 2-min video walkthrough if easier than a call?

### "Warm intro" ask (to mutual contacts)

> Hi {FirstName}, quick ask — I noticed you're connected to {Name} at {Company}. I'm building a WBC3 ops app for workboat operators and would love a 15-min chat with them. Could you forward the note below, or would a direct intro feel OK?

### LinkedIn post — "why I built this"

> After {X years} as a maritime pilot, I watched too many workboat operators running their WBC3 compliance on Excel and paper.
>
> Certs expire without warning. Maintenance logs get lost. MCA audits become fire drills.
>
> I've started a company called Fleetskipper to fix it — a simple ops app built for WBC3 operators, by someone who's actually been on the water.
>
> We're in pilot with a couple of UK operators now. If you run a workboat fleet and you're tired of the paperwork, message me.

### LinkedIn post — "practical, value-add"

> 3 things MCA surveyors consistently check under WBC3 that operators underprepare for:
>
> 1. Crew competency matrix — not just "are they certified," but "is the evidence organised and current?"
> 2. Planned maintenance records with dates, signatures, and deviations documented.
> 3. Risk assessments reviewed within the last 12 months (yes, someone will ask).
>
> Seen it time and again. Keep them close at hand — it saves you a bad day.

## 5. Weekly rhythm (so it actually happens)

If you can only do one hour on outreach per day, protect this hour.

| Day  | 60-min focus                                         |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Mon  | Research 10 new contacts; mark priorities for the week |
| Tue  | 5 LinkedIn messages + 5 emails                        |
| Wed  | 5 LinkedIn messages + 5 emails                        |
| Thu  | Follow-ups; take any discovery calls                  |
| Fri  | Update the tracker; publish LinkedIn post; review pipeline |

A month of this and you'll have ~40 companies contacted, ~5–8 replies, and 2–3 meetings booked. That's a real pipeline.
