**Question 6:** What level of customization is included?

**ANSWER:**
- [ ] Fully bespoke - built from scratch for each vessel
- [x] **Template-based with substantial customization** ✅ THIS IS OUR APPROACH
- [ ] Pre-built with vessel-specific details added

**Our Approach - "Universal Templates + Smart Engines":**

The templates are UNIVERSAL starting points designed to work for any workboat company (tiny to large). We then use automation engines to:

1. **Populate company data** - Company name, person ashore, vessel names, crew roles, office locations
2. **Scale to operation size** - 2 crew vs 30 crew, 1 vessel vs 10 vessels
3. **Customize for vessel type** - CTV operations vs tug operations vs dive support
4. **Adapt procedures** - Vessel-specific equipment, operational patterns, route-specific risks

**Customization Process:**
1. Client completes onboarding questionnaire (company details, vessel specs, operations)
2. AI engine populates templates with client data
3. Consultant reviews and adds vessel-specific customizations
4. Client reviews draft and provides feedback
5. Final adjustments and delivery
6. Word documents provided so client can further edit if needed

**Philosophy:** Start with proven WBC3-compliant templates, then customize intelligently rather than reinventing the wheel every time.

## Section 4: Client Needs & Pain Points

**Question 8:** What do clients ask for most often that we DON'T currently offer?

**ANSWER:**
1. **Digital platform/app** - Clients want digital tools instead of Word/PDF/Excel. They want mobile access, automatic reminders, digital signatures. **(This is why we're building SeaReady App!)**

2. **Pre-built maintenance schedules** - Vessel-specific planned maintenance systems with actual manufacturer maintenance intervals for engines, hydraulics, safety equipment, etc.

3. **Crew management system** - Digital crew records, certificate expiry tracking, scheduling, leave management integrated with competence tracking

4. **Form templates** - Pre-formatted forms for near-miss reporting, incident investigation, toolbox talks, risk assessment sign-offs

5. **Training materials/videos** - Ready-made crew training materials, induction videos, procedure walkthroughs instead of just written documents

---

**Question 9:** What parts of the SMS do clients struggle with most after delivery?

**ANSWER:**
1. **Keeping it up to date** - Crew changes, vessel modifications, new equipment → SMS gets out of date quickly. Clients don't have time to maintain it.

2. **Remembering certificate expiries** - Crew certificates, vessel certificates, safety equipment inspections. Excel trackers work but require manual checking. Miss a renewal = non-compliance.

3. **Conducting and recording drills** - WBC3 requires "regular routine" drills but clients forget to schedule them, struggle with recording them properly, and don't know what "regularly routinely" means in practice.

4. **Risk assessment discipline** - Completing risk assessments BEFORE new tasks/operations. Crew see it as paperwork, not safety. Gets skipped under time pressure.

5. **Document version control** - When procedures change, ensuring all crew have latest version. Multiple versions floating around = confusion and non-compliance.
